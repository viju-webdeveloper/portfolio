Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

A True Hero of India

Subhas Chandra Bose was President of the Indian National Congress in 1938 and 1939.

Here's a time line of Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose life:
  • 1897   -  Born in Cuttack-in what is today the state of Odisha in India.
  • 1902   -  Entered the Baptist Mission's Protestant European School in Cuttack
  • 1912   -  Secured the second position in the matriculation examination conducted under the auspices of the University of Calcutta.
  • 1918   -  Got the First Class with honours in philosophy, placing second among all philosophy students in Calcutta University.
  • 1927   -  Became general secretary of the Congress party and worked with Jawaharlal Nehru for independence.
  • 1930   -  Bose was again arrested and jailed for civil disobedience, this time he emerged to become Mayor of Calcutta.
  • 1935   -  He also researched and wrote the book "The Indian Struggle", which covered the country's independence movement in the years 1920–1934. The British government banned the book in the colony out of fears that it would encourage unrest.
  • 1937   -  Bose stated his opinion that the INC "should be organised on the broadest anti-imperialist front with the two-fold objective of winning political freedom and the establishment of a socialist regime."By 1938 Bose had become a leader of national stature and agreed to accept nomination as Congress President. He stood for unqualified Swaraj (self-governance), including the use of force against the British.
  • 1943   -  He led the Indian National Army or Azad Hind Fauj in to free India from British rule. Netaji's Nationalist ideas inspired million of people to fight for the freedom of India.
  • 1944   -  Most famous quote was "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!" In this, he urged the people of India to join him in his fight against the British Raj.
  • 1944   -  In a speech broadcast by the Azad Hind Radio from Singapore, Bose addressed Mahatma Gandhi as the "Father of the Nation" and asked for his blessings and good wishes for the war he was fighting. This was the first time that Gandhi was referred to by this appellation.
  • 1945   -  He reportedly died at a hospital in Taiwan as a result of injuries from a plane crash.
